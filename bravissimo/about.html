ABOUT

HISTORY


New York in the 1890's had almost everything... except for a place where an opera lover, after a night of Verdi or Puccini, could relax, play a Neapolitan card game called "scopa" and drink a cup or two of espresso.


This situation was remedied when our great grandfather, Enrico Scoppa and great grand-uncle, Antonio Ferrara, opera impresario and showman, opened a cafe called Caffe A. Ferrara. Caruso thought the coffee marvelous but especially loved the cookies and cakes.


After World War I, our grandfather, Peter Lepore, a nephew of Antonio Ferrara, had stowed away on a ship bound for New York. At the beginning of the 1929 Depression, Peter married the daughter of Enrico Scoppa. He would spend the rest of his life at Ferrara's, transforming a charming neighborhood cafe into a world famous shrine of delicious tastes.


Times were tough and in order to make sure there was very little waste, they began to bake two, three and sometimes four times a day, small batches of cookies and cakes. These difficult circumstances were a blessing in disguise.


Because of the frequency with which they baked, Ferrara acquired a great reputation for freshness. This reputation and the fact that the Lepores were almost as devoted to their business as to each other, enabled Caffe Ferrara to grow and prosper.


Today, Ferrara is still a family owned business operated by the fifth generation.


In 1986, brothers Peter and Ernest Lepore headed west to stake their own claim and add their own shingle to the family's storied food history. They opened Bravo Cucina on Santa Monica's famed Third Street Promenade to rave reviews and a loyal following of regulars.


2002 marked the arrival of Bravo Pizzeria, a New York style slice joint on Santa Monica's lively Main Street that featured some of the same pasta and salad dishes that made Bravo Cucina famous. Many wonderful memories and friendships have been created over hot slices on the pizzeria's eight-hundred square foot outdoor dining patio.


In 2013, we re-introduced ourselves as Bravissimo Pizzeria and Cafe, with the same great food, some new items, and the addition of fresh gelato and even more of Ferrrara's world famous desserts and pastries.
